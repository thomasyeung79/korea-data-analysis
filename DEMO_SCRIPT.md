# Korea Compass Demo Script

Use this script for a 5 to 8 minute portfolio walkthrough.

## 1. Homepage

### What to show

Open the Korea Compass homepage and point out the workflow cards.

### What to say

"Korea Compass is a Study, Work, Live, Korean Learning, and AI planning app for international users considering South Korea. The flow starts with exploration, moves into planning, and ends with an exportable Korea Life Plan."

### Why it matters

It frames the product as a complete planning workflow rather than a single calculator.

## 2. Explore Korea

### What to show

Open Explore Korea and switch between Overview, Cities, Culture, History, Cost of Living, and Quick Facts.

### What to say

"This module gives users a structured starting point before they make decisions. The content comes from the shared Knowledge Base instead of hardcoded page data."

### Why it matters

It shows the product can support discovery and planning from the same data layer.

## 3. Profile Center

### What to show

Fill or review Study, Career, and Living profile fields.

### What to say

"The Profile Center captures reusable planning inputs. Study, career, living, city recommendation, and life plan features can all use the same user profile."

### Why it matters

It demonstrates product coherence and avoids duplicate user input.

## 4. City Recommendation

### What to show

Generate city rankings and inspect the best city card, score breakdown, and chart.

### What to say

"The city recommendation engine combines study fit, career fit, living fit, cost, language fit, and lifestyle fit. It is a transparent scoring model rather than a black box."

### Why it matters

It turns user profile data into a practical decision-support output.

## 5. Study Cost

### What to show

Run a study cost estimate and show the breakdown chart and explanation.

### What to say

"The Study Cost Calculator estimates monthly and annual costs across tuition, housing, food, transport, insurance, and miscellaneous expenses. It supports bilingual explanations and export."

### Why it matters

Budget planning is one of the first real constraints for studying abroad.

## 6. Career Analyzer

### What to show

Select different roles such as Backend Developer, Marketing Specialist, Registered Nurse, or English Teacher.

### What to say

"The Career Analyzer is role-specific. Skills, cities, Korean requirements, visa pathway, competitiveness, and preparation plans change by career path."

### Why it matters

It shows the product has moved beyond a generic IT-only template.

## 7. Korean Learning

### What to show

Open Study Korean, Career Korean, Living Korean, and TOPIK Planner tabs.

### What to say

"Korean Learning is a support feature, not a separate language app. It focuses on expressions and situations users actually encounter while studying, working, or living in Korea."

### Why it matters

It supports the core product journey without changing the product scope.

## 8. AI Korea Life Plan

### What to show

Generate a Korea Life Plan and show recommendation, best city, study path, career path, living plan, risks, visa pathway, and action plans.

### What to say

"The AI Korea Life Plan combines profile data, city scoring, cost estimates, career signals, and planning logic into an exportable report. It can run with a local fallback, so no paid API is required for the demo."

### Why it matters

This is the product's main synthesis moment.

## 9. Knowledge Base Status

### What to show

Open Knowledge Base Status and show total JSON files, metadata coverage, source coverage, confidence distribution, and validation issues.

### What to say

"The Knowledge Base Status page is an internal quality view. It tracks metadata completeness, source status, official coverage, mock coverage, and confidence distribution."

### Why it matters

It proves the product is prepared for future official data integration instead of relying on untraceable static content.
